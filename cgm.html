Physiological Time-Series Modeling for Continuous Glucose Monitoring

This project focuses on the development of machine learning methods for continuous physiological monitoring. By leveraging deep learning and time-series alignment techniques, the study aims to improve glucose prediction accuracy and reduce false alarms in continuous glucose monitoring systems.

Research Motivation

Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) systems generate large volumes of physiological time-series data. Accurate prediction of glucose dynamics is essential for improving diabetes management, reducing adverse events, and supporting personalized healthcare interventions.

Focus Areas
  • Physiological Time-Series Analysis
  • Deep Learning
  • Digital Health
  • Predictive Modeling
  • Personalized Monitoring

Research Objectives

  • Predict future glucose levels from continuous monitoring data
  • Improve personalized modeling of physiological dynamics
  • Reduce false alarms through anomaly detection
  • Enhance reliability of digital health monitoring systems

Methods

  • Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM)
  • Dynamic Time Warping (DTW)
  • Anomaly Detection Algorithms
  • Time-Series Feature Engineering

The framework combines sequence modeling with temporal alignment techniques to better capture individual physiological patterns and improve prediction robustness.

Results

The proposed approach demonstrated improved monitoring accuracy and alarm precision compared with traditional monitoring strategies.

By incorporating anomaly detection mechanisms, the system reduced false alerts and increased reliability for continuous physiological monitoring applications.

Technical Skills

  • Python
  • Deep Learning
  • BiLSTM
  • Time-Series Analysis
  • Dynamic Time Warping (DTW)
  • Anomaly Detection

Research Significance

This project highlights the potential of machine learning methods for personalized physiological monitoring and digital health applications. The work contributes to ongoing efforts to develop intelligent systems capable of supporting real-time clinical decision-making and patient self-management.

Future Directions

Future work may incorporate multimodal physiological signals, wearable sensor data, and personalized adaptive learning frameworks to further improve predictive performance and clinical utility.
